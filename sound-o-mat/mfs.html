Discography Biography Music for Seances


The Music for Seances series was a joint project between Rob Vaughn at The Sound-O-Mat and Clark Whittington at Art*O*Mat from 2003-2004.

Each of the 13 releases contained a small rectangular CDR decorated as a vintage talking board, a 1940's influenced style "insert" info poster and a tiny planchette in a custom box, each with an ambient track by various artists. 50 copies of each release were produced and vended by Art*O*Mat and an additional 30 copies were available for sale directly from The Sound-O-Mat.

All copies were sold out by 2011.

Of all the releases I've put out on my tiny label, I'm most proud of this series, despite the extreme cost in time, money and effort. It was a crazy idea that I didn't think possible, but with Clark's boundless enthusiasm and support, we made it happen.

I came across Clark's Art*O*Mat project while I was surfing the 'net looking into various things-O-Mat history. I immediately wanted to be involved in his brilliant art project. I joined his repotoire of artists, purchased the basic materials, and set about doing runs of pieces based on old stamps, currency and maps.

I'd always been interested in the Art Deco & Mid-Century influenced design of talking (aka Ouija) boards and had accumulated a collection of them, as well as using thrift store finds of the Parker Bros. boards as endcaps for the old steel shelves I used for my record collection.

At some point it dawned on me that the A*O*M boxes were a similar profile to the ones talking boards came in, and I started thinking about doing a series of miniature ones. Around the same time I was researching card-shaped CDRs for a project I was working on, and came across a company making custom rectangular "business card" shaped blank CDRs that were small enough to just barely fit in an A*O*M vending machine, angled corner to opposite corner.

Thus a really bad idea was hatched.

I ran the idea past Clark and with his go-ahead I quickly put together a package design based on vintage boards with an info sheet based on "insert sized" movie posters that originated in the 1950's and were common until the early '80's. Those posters were "tall", 14x36 inches in a 0.389 ratio with the popular style of text in mixed and wildly varying fonts.
